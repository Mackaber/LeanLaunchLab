TIP 1: Think about failing.

What are all the ways your business could fail? List out these reasons and create hypotheses from them.

Example: Ipod

  • Failure Reason 1: Can't get music deals with OEMs
  • Failure Reason 2: Can't design up to Apple standards
  • Failure Reason 3: Can't fit 1000 songs in one device

Create hypotheses for each one:

  • Hypothesis 1: Music OEMs will license their collections to us
  • Hypothesis 2: We are able to innovate on music player hardware designs
  • Hypothesis 3: Technology exists for high-storage at low cost
Credit: http://davidkullmann.com/2012/01/14/how-to-find-your-lean-startup-hypothesis/

TIP 2: Create a Problem/Solution Hypothesis.

Your customers have problem X. What's your proposed solution and how will you validate it short of actually building it? Try structuring your hypothesis like this:

[SOLUTION] will solve/help [CUSTOMER PROBLEM]
[SOLUTION] will increase/decrease [METRIC]

Examples:

  • Online chat widget solves customer support problem
  • Adding social features will increase user engagement by 20%
  • Sending reminder emails will increase retention by 50%

TIP 3: Start your hypothesis with "I believe"

Examples:

  • I believe fitness enthusiasts will share their workouts on facebook and twitter twice per week
  • I believe online shoppers are heavily influenced by social recommendations
  • I believe feature X will drive conversions up 5%
